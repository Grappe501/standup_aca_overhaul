# **ACA for the People**

## **Legislative Counsel--Ready Redline Outline**

(How this fits into U.S. Code and existing ACA provisions)

## **PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT**

This outline shows:

- Where ACA for the People amends existing law

- What sections are added vs. modified

- How to minimize drafting friction

- Which titles can move independently

- Which provisions should be clean inserts vs. rewrites

Legislative counsel can use this as:

- a drafting map,

- a checklist,

- or a modular menu.

## **OVERALL STRUCTURE**

Bill Type:

Amendments to the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (PPACA) + new freestanding titles

Committees of Jurisdiction (Primary):

- House Energy & Commerce

- House Ways & Means

- Senate HELP

- Senate Finance

# **TITLE I --- FINDINGS, PURPOSES, AND DEFINITIONS (NEW)**

### **Action**

NEW TITLE added at the front of the Act

### **Reason**

- Avoids reopening ACA definitions scattered across titles

- Creates a unified interpretive lens for courts

- Anchors affordability, backbone, and enforcement intent

### **Contents**

- Congressional findings

- Statement of purpose

- Definitions for:

  - "Healthcare Backbone"

  - "Affordability Cap"

  - "Essential Access Provider"

  - "Administrative Waste"

  - "Delay or Denial"

# **TITLE II --- HEALTHCARE BACKBONE (NEW CORE TITLE)**

### **Action**

NEW TITLE inserted after ACA Title I (Quality, Affordable Health Care for All Americans)

### **U.S. Code Placement**

- New Part added to 42 U.S.C. Â§18021 et seq.

### **Key Drafting Instructions**

- Do not repeal ACA Marketplace structure

- Backbone is an additional statutory plan option

- Eligibility and benefits defined in statute

- HHS role limited to implementation mechanics

### **Sections**

- Establishment of Healthcare Backbone Plan

- Eligibility and enrollment

- Benefits floor

- Relationship to private insurance

- Federal fallback authority

# **TITLE IV --- PROVIDER PAYMENT & RURAL STABILIZATION (NEW TITLE)**

### **Action**

NEW TITLE, but cross-referenced to Medicare/Medicaid authorities

### **Drafting Strategy**

- Avoid reopening Medicare fee schedules

- Use alternative payment authority

- Create new designation: "Essential Access Provider"

### **Sections**

- Access-based payments

- Rural hospital stabilization

- Emergency & maternity protection

- Administrative simplification for providers

# **TITLE V --- INSURANCE MARKET RULES & POWER REBALANCING**

(MODIFIES ACA Title I, Part II)

### **Action**

TARGETED AMENDMENTS to ACA insurer rules

### **Key Sections to Amend**

- 42 U.S.C. Â§300gg-1 et seq.

- Network adequacy standards

- Prior authorization restrictions

### **Drafting Note**

- Use bright-line prohibitions, not standards

- Avoid "reasonable" or "appropriate" without criteria

- Explicit penalty triggers

# **TITLE VI --- ADMINISTRATIVE SIMPLIFICATION (NEW TITLE)**

### **Action**

NEW TITLE, minimal cross-code conflict

### **Drafting Strategy**

- Build on existing HHS/CMS authorities

- Standardization \> new programs

- Emphasize efficiency and cost reduction

### **Sections**

- No-wrong-door enrollment

- Standardized billing

- Claims timelines

- Interoperability standards

# **TITLE VII --- PRESCRIPTION DRUG & PBM REFORM**

(MODIFIES PHSA + ERISA + Internal Revenue Code)

### **Action**

MULTI-CODE AMENDMENTS (targeted)

### **Drafting Strategy**

- Clear prohibitions (pass-through, spread pricing)

- Avoid rate-setting

- Ensure ERISA-safe federal authority

### **Sections**

- Rebate pass-through

- Spread pricing ban

- Formulary transparency

- Pharmacy protections

# **TITLE IX --- OVERSIGHT, ENFORCEMENT, ACCOUNTABILITY (NEW)**

### **Action**

NEW TITLE

### **Drafting Strategy**

- Create independent Oversight Commission

- Mandatory enforcement timelines

- Public reporting

- Whistleblower protections

### **Key Feature**

- Enforcement duties written as "shall", not "may"

# **TITLE X --- DURABILITY ADDENDUM (NEW APPENDIX)**

### **Action**

NEW FINAL TITLE / APPENDIX

### **Purpose**

- Lock in enforcement

- Prevent slow-walking

- Create statutory defaults

- Enable judicial review

### **Sections**

- Nondiscretionary implementation

- Default rules for missed deadlines

- Limited private right of action

- Anti-erosion clause

- Enhanced severability

## **MODULAR PASSAGE STRATEGY (IMPORTANT FOR COUNSEL)**

These titles can move independently if needed:

- Drug reform (Title VII)

- Administrative simplification (Title VI)

- Affordability caps (Title III)

- Rural stabilization (Title IV)

The backbone (Title II) is the structural keystone but can be phased.
