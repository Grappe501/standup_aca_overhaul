## **What Title III Means for You**

### **The Healthcare Backbone --- finally putting you first**

(Plain-English explainer for the National Healthcare Backbone Act)

## **The short version (2 minutes)**

Title III creates something America has never had:

> A guaranteed healthcare foundation that works for everyone --- no matter your job, income, or ZIP code.

It gives you:

- a reliable health plan you can always choose,

- clear rules about what's covered,

- limits on what you pay,

- and protection from insurance company games.

Private insurance doesn't disappear --- but it stops running the system.

## **The problem this fixes**

Right now, your healthcare depends on:

- where you work,

- how much you earn,

- what state you live in,

- and how well you understand fine print.

If you lose a job, move, get sick, or earn a little more money, your coverage can change --- or vanish.

Insurance companies decide:

- what's covered,

- when it's covered,

- and how hard it is to use.

That's backwards.

## **What the "Healthcare Backbone" actually is**

Think of the healthcare backbone like public infrastructure.

Just like:

- roads,

- electricity,

- or clean water,

it sets basic rules everyone can rely on.

The healthcare backbone is:

- a baseline health plan available to everyone,

- with simple benefits,

- fair prices,

- and clear protections.

You don't have to use it --- but it's always there.

## **Why this lowers costs**

Healthcare gets cheaper when:

- prices are predictable,

- waste is removed,

- and companies can't game the system.

The backbone:

- reduces paperwork,

- stabilizes coverage,

- limits cost-shifting,

- and forces honest competition.

That lowers premiums, deductibles, and bills --- without cutting care.

## **Why this matters even if you're "doing okay"**

Even if you have good insurance today:

- your costs are still rising,

- your coverage can still change,

- your hospital could still close,

- and one serious illness could still upend your finances.

The backbone is insurance against the system itself.

## **The bottom line**

Title III does one powerful thing:

You get:

- stability instead of anxiety,

- clarity instead of confusion,

- protection instead of risk.

That's what healthcare should feel like.
